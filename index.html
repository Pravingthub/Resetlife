The plan

One problem, not four. Less alcohol, less liquid sugar, more walking, more protein, better sleep — and the liver, lipids, blood pressure and fat all move on the same levers.

Liver & lipids — fast wins
Cut alcohol hard — it's hitting your liver, triglycerides and blood pressure at once.
Kill liquid sugar (juice, soft drinks, sweet coffee). Fructose routes straight to liver fat.
Soluble fiber daily: oats, dal, beans, barley. Psyllium husk 5–10g pulls LDL down.
Swap fried/ghee for olive oil + a daily handful of nuts.
Coffee early in the shift is liver-protective — just not within 6h of sleep.
Triglycerides move fastest — 2–4 weeks. LDL over 4–12 weeks. Liver fat clears in weeks to months once the inputs stop.
Supplement stack
B-complex with thiamine (B1) — standard for heavy drinkers; alcohol depletes it.
Magnesium (glycinate, at night) — sleep, blood pressure, insulin sensitivity.
Psyllium husk 5–10g/day — cheap, proven LDL and blood-sugar help.
Omega-3 / fish oil — modest liver and triglyceride benefit.
Avoid "fat burners" and high-dose green-tea-extract — liver-toxic.
Caution: paracetamol + alcohol is hard on the liver. Run the stack past a pharmacist.
Weekly training
Order every session
1 · Warm-up (6–8 min) — plate or easy march, joint circles, cat-cow, 10 squats + 10 bridges + 20 light punches. Warm, not tired.
2 · Boxing / skill — while you're fresh; coordination fades with fatigue.
3 · Strength — the muscle-keeping work.
4 · Steady cardio (treadmill) — forgiving, so it goes when you're tired.
5 · Cool-down (5 min) — easy walk down, stretches, box-breathing 4-4-4-4 ×6.
The vibration plate bookends a session (warm-up primer or cool-down aid) — it's never a middle block.

Zone 2 & heart rate
Treadmill incline 3–6 %, brisk walk — never run.
Talk test: can talk, can't sing. Can sing → raise incline; can't talk → slow down.
Helio target ~110–128 bpm (≈60–70% of ~183 max). RPE 5–6. Boxing/TT sit higher at ~137–155.
Autoregulate to the night shift
Slept under 5 h, or resting HR ≥7 bpm above baseline? Downgrade a hard day to a Zone 2 walk or Pilates.
Drank the night before? No hard session — walk or rest.
Feeling beat but okay? Do the warm-up + first block, then decide.
Scaling down on a bad day is training intelligence, not weakness. Stop and seek care for chest pain, severe breathlessness, dizziness, or an irregular heartbeat.

Circadian & sleep
Bright light during the shift to stay alert; dim / blue-block on the drive home.
Day-sleep cave: blackout, cool, silent, eye mask. Same sleep timing even on days off.
Caffeine off ~6h before sleep.
Front-load food: biggest meal before the shift, lightest at 2–3 AM — your night-body handles carbs worst then.
Reading your gauges (no labs)
Waist — best visceral-fat gauge. Under ~90 cm is where risk drops for South Asian men.
Blood pressure cuff — the one "lab number" you can read at home daily.
Resting HR on waking — whole-system recovery proxy.
Soft signals: morning energy, afternoon crashes, sleep quality, waistband.
Safety — read once
Drinking heavily every day? Taper over 1–2 weeks — don't quit cold turkey. Withdrawal can be dangerous.
See someone now if: yellow eyes/skin, vomiting blood or black stools, severe upper-right belly pain, abdomen/leg swelling, or confusion.
These are unlikely — listed once so they're in your head. The bloodwork door stays open whenever you want it.
